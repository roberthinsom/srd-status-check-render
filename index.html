Common Reasons for SASSA SRD Grant Rejections and How to Avoid Them

1. Failure to Meet Basic Eligibility Criteria

One of the most common reasons for SRD grant rejection is not meeting the basic eligibility requirements. To qualify, applicants must meet certain criteria, including:

  • Being a South African citizen, permanent resident, or refugee.
  • Being between the ages of 18 and 60 years old.
  • Not receiving any other form of government assistance (such as UIF, disability, or pension grants).
  • Not exceeding the income threshold set by SASSA.

Tip: Use the SRD Status Check tool on the SASSA website to confirm your eligibility before applying.

2. Income and Financial Means Testing

SASSA conducts means testing to determine if your income qualifies you for the SRD grant. If your income exceeds the set limit, your application will be rejected. This includes earnings from informal work, side jobs, or financial support from household members.

Tip: Ensure that you report all sources of income accurately. Household members' income will also be considered in the assessment.

3. Incorrect or Incomplete Application Details

Submitting incomplete or incorrect details on your application, such as missing personal information, incorrect ID numbers, or missing documentation, is a common reason for rejection.

Tip: Double-check your application details and ensure all required documents are uploaded in the correct format.

4. Bank Account Issues

If the bank account details you provide are incorrect, closed, or not in your name, your SRD grant application will be rejected. SASSA requires an active account in your name to process payments.

Tip: Verify that your bank account is active and that the name matches the details on your ID.

5. Failure to Update Personal Information

If your personal details change after you’ve submitted your application (e.g., contact details, address, or financial status), and you don’t update SASSA, it may cause delays or rejection.

Tip: Use the SRD Status Check tool to ensure your details are up to date.

6. Use of Invalid or Ineligible Documents

Submitting expired or forged documents will lead to rejection. SASSA requires valid, up-to-date documents to verify your identity and eligibility.

Tip: Make sure all documents (ID, proof of residence, etc.) are current and legible.

7. Failure to Respond to SASSA’s Requests for Additional Information

If SASSA requests additional information or documents and you fail to respond within the given timeframe, your application may be rejected.

Tip: Stay on top of your application status by using the SRD Status Check tool and responding promptly to SASSA’s requests.

8. Technical or System Errors

Occasionally, system errors may cause an application to be incorrectly processed. This can result in delays or rejections, even if the application is correct.

Tip: Ensure you apply on the official SASSA website and check the SRD Status Check tool for updates if you suspect a technical issue.

9. Previous Overpayment or Fraudulent Claims

If you were previously overpaid or found to have committed fraud, this could lead to the rejection of future applications for the SRD grant.

Tip: Be honest when applying for the SRD grant. If you’ve had issues with previous claims, resolve them with SASSA before reapplying.
